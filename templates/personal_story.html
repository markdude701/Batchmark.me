A Night in College

 


A Short story about typical night as a university student

Written for Creative Writing at Penn State Behrend

Mark Makowski

Exercise Exercise

 

There is a lot of people around me, my phone is dead, and I want to go home. Surrounded by so many drunken idiots, I stay close to my friends, because the thought of hanging out with them sounded much better than going to this “Party”.

Is this what the cool college kids are doing now-a-days? Do people, my age, actually find this entertaining? Granted, I am older than most of the kids that go to school here. But, I never really had a drunken, “LET’S GET WILD” phase of college, like most kids, I believe, do. I would love to see an actual metric percentage of the number of kids who don’t take part in the “fun” versus the ones who do. Is there a college stereotype there? I wouldn’t be able to tell you, because I don’t really fall into it. But let’s be real here, there may, or may have not, been times where I might have thrown up six times in a random bathroom at University Park, Pennsylvania during Halloween time. After that experience, I realized alcohol was not my cup of tea.

I still go to a few of the parties, that my friends are interested in going to, but for the most part, I really hate parties. I have had fun at some parties, but most of the time, you show up, you walk through many groups of sweaty, hot, hormones-infused late-teenagers, usually to an even warmer part of the house, where the drinks are located. I am friends with most of the hosts, so getting a drink isn’t a problem. I look around, at the dark room, filled of young women and disgusting, or “redneck” guys, all appear  to be looking to be several years younger than I am, with the exception of the hosts and a few of my friends. Some part of me misses the moment when I was just a nobody there, and I was a young hooligan, just hanging out. Now, as time is going by, I realize how much of a fad this is. I realize that people, this age, tend to just get drunk with their friends and party because that is what everyone is doing. As I continue to look around the room, to verify my judgement, the expressions that are one everyone’s face is like going to see an M. Knight. Shyamalan movie and expecting it to be good. Everyone is looking like they are trying to enjoy it, but really, we’re all dying inside.

I know that made a turn for the worst, but really. Everyone looks like they are having fun, but are we really? My three friends and I take a look around, and converse with the few remaining people that we know. We talk about usual the usual college garbage, such as “How’s class”, “How is ….”, you get the picture. I don’t really say much, being that I don’t try to participate in the mindlessness, but I still say Hi and move on. I just want to walk home. I live around the block, and it is less than a two minute walk. I just want to go home, and relax in my moderately temperate house with these attractive girls I brought to this party. But, we stand around and continue to talk about classes and that stuff.

After a minute, the friends we were talking to leave, and our group continues to stand in place. We weren’t the only group there, the house was filled with drunken, gross people, but standing in a group, out of the way, seems to work. I look around at all the people, and notice some of them looking back. I continue talking to my friends and just ignore the people I made eye contact with.

Now, after some time, we’re just standing there, trying to figure out what to do. I am just bored out of my mind; this place is a toilet. During the inception of that thought, a kid, around eighteen or so, comes stumbling up the stairs, and goes towards where we were standing. WIthout hesitation, this kid throws up on the ground, on my shoes, and on a girls leg and shoes. I’ve never met her before but she looked a bit more frustrated than I am. She starts freaking out on him, in a very unexpected manner. She pulled him by the arm, towards the outside door, and continues to say “I’m gonna kick your ass”. As you would expect, my friends and I tried to follow.

As we got to the door, one of the host stops us and says that there are too many people outside, and everyone should stay inside. My friends and I are befuddled, with how to respond. We go aside and talk about our plans. The doorman said we can’t go outside unless we’re leaving. I gave them an alternative to hang out at my place, down the block, and they thought it would be nice  to, at least, get outside for a little bit.

We go to the doorman, one of my older friends, and tell him we’re walking back to my place. We walk outside, through the groups of cigarette smoking college students, say goodbye to some of our friends, and continue to my place.

Awesome.
